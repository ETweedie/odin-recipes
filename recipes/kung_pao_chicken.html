Kung Pao Chicken recipe

Description

Do you love restaurant style Kung Pao Chicken? Well if you do, you are in the right place. This recipe will let you make that restaurant style Kung Pao Chicken at home!

This recipe will be a head turner for anyone you make this for. They will think you bought it from the restaurant and passed it off as home made. This recipe will be great to have for those days when you are craving Chinese food but don't want to order out. Overall, this will be a great recipe to add to the books, since you will love it and so will your guests.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 tablespoons white wine, divided
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce, divided
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cut into chunks
  • 1 ounce hot chili paste
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
  • 1 (8 ounce) can water chestnuts
  • 4 ounces chopped peanuts
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients
  2. Combine water and cornstarch in a cup, set aside
  3. Combine 1 tablespoon wine, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, and 1 tablespoon cornstarch/water mixture in a large glass bowl. Add chicken pieces and toss to coat. Cover the dish and refrigerate for about 30 minutes.
  4. Combine remaining 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon sesame oil, and remaining cornstarch/water mixture in medium bowl. Whisk in chili paste, brown sugar, and vinegar. Add water chestnuts, peanuts, green onions, and garlic and toss to coat.
  5. Transfer water chestnut mixture to medium skillet. Heat slowly over medium heat until aromatic.
  6. Meanwhile, transfer chicken from marinade into large skillet; cook over medium-high heat, stirring, until chicken is cooked through and juices run clear.
  7. Combine water chestnut mixture and sauteed chicken together in one skillet. Adjust heat and simmer together until sauce thickens.
